Overview

This embedded systems device is responsible for delivering stable high voltage power to rocket hardware that requires it.

Situation

For our most recent liquids rocket design, our team SARP, requires an extensive high-power system to power the onboard electronics and interface with other subsystems. Launching a rocket requires our design to be capable of seamlessly switching from DC ground power to a battery backup.

Solution

As an avionics member of SARP on the Power subteam, I collaborated closely with the Flight Computer, Radio, and ground system subteams to design a high voltage power system that appropriately interfaces with their respective design requirements. All the while focusing on creating a robust design that can be used down the line. For our design, I researched and implemented the BQ25820 charge controller, which allows for the rocket to seamlessly switch between ground and battery power. Additionally this charge controller allows our team to charge the batteries while connected into ground power. This component's design is crucial to our system, and has diverse power inputs and outputs for future rocket designs.
